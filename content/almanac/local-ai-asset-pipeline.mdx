The illustrations across this folio were grown on a single consumer GPU. No
cloud queue, no per-image bill, just a repeatable pipeline sitting on my own
machine. Here is roughly how the plates came to be.

## Generate to a known background

Every prompt ends with the same instruction: a flat, plain, solid cream
background at exactly the page colour. Consistency at the generation step makes
everything downstream easier. Even so, I never trust the background to match. I
always cut it out.

## Cut out, do not colour-key blindly

The keying step is where naive pipelines ruin good art. Mine samples the border
colour, builds a colour-distance mask, then keeps only the regions that connect
to the image border. That last part is the trick: it protects a cream envelope
or a pale petal in the middle of the subject from being erased along with the
background.

- Alpha ramp with a gentle gamma, so edges feather instead of jagging.
- Crop to the alpha bounding box with a small pad.
- Resize, then WebP at a high quality.

## Knowing when to stop

The hardest part is not the tooling. It is resisting the urge to regenerate one
more time. At some point the plate is good enough to press, and the folio wants
finishing more than it wants a marginally better leaf.
